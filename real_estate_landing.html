Understanding the National Real Estate License Exam

The National Real Estate License Exam is a crucial step in launching your real estate career. This comprehensive examination tests your knowledge of federal real estate laws, ethical practices, and fundamental real estate principles that apply across all states.

Exam Format

The national portion typically consists of 80-100 multiple-choice questions covering contracts, property ownership, and federal regulations.

Passing Score

Most states require a score of 70% or higher on the national portion to qualify for licensure.

Time Limit

Candidates are generally given 3.5 to 4 hours to complete both the national and state-specific portions of the exam.
